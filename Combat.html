Combat Mechanics, RPG & TBS Combined

Role-Playing Game Mechanics

Attack Mechanics

  • Overall, attacking should be much like MMClassic, with a few exceptions: arrow attacks should do more damage thru a combo of might/accuracy effect, buff spells & new hit system (below); and, magic-heavy/exclusive classes should be able to cast a spell as a ranged attack thru a staff or wand.
  • Staff/wand attack: casts the basic DD spell of a selected magic school at the target, reduced sp cost as follows: Bas - 20%/Adv - 30%/Exp - 50%/Mas - 75%/GM - 100%. Must know magic school to use that type of atk.

Hit Rate & Bonus Mechanics

  • Overall, the hitrate should revolve around a critical-like system of 5 possible hits: very weak, weak, average, strong & very strong.
  • Tentative names: Glancing Blow, Weak Hit, Average Hit, Critical Hit, Slayer Strike
  • Default damage modifiers: 0.25, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, 4.0
  • Armor Class & Hit Bonus should revolve almost entirely around modifying the chances of which of the 5 occurs, and/or the damage modifiers, rather than manipulation of the underlying damage formula. Same goes for Magic Resistances.
  • For physical attacks, the calculation should be calculated based on the ratio of attacker's Hit Bonus with the defender's AC, on a -4 to +4 scale. Tentatively, -4 means AC 400% greater than Hit Bonus, -3 means AC 300% greater than Hit Bonus, +4 means Hit Bonus 400% greater than AC, etc. At 0, all 5 hit possibilities are open. At +1, worst possibility disappears & chances grow for better ones. At +2, the next weakest possibility disappears leaving Average as the worst-case scenario, with Critical being the most likely. At +3, Average is totally ruled out, leaving only Critical or Slayer Strike as options, on a sliding scale that tilts towards Slayer the further towards +4. At +4 & above, Slayer Strike is guaranteed. The reverse happens with symmetrical chances for negative values.
  • The hit modifier should be multiplicative & occur after the weapon damage is calculated, along with any ele weakness/armor skill/etc. modifiers.

Turn-Based Game Mechanics

All combat mechanics such as turn structure, speed calculations, etc. should be copied wholesale from classic except where noted.

Morale

  • Same -3 to +3 scale as H3C generally (GM Leadership opens up +4, GM Demoralize opens up -4), but two positive & 2 negative outcomes, rather than just 1 of each. Also, Master Leadership/Demoralization effects cancel out, but GM effects override Master.
  • Positive: Minor - 50% extra movement hexes for the unit this turn or 50% bonus atk spd if extra movement unused. Rolls for & is triggered at unit turn start, indicated in info box with some indication (e.g. [7]) that distinguishes the one-turn nature of the effect. Major - Extra full turn of movement/atk for the unit. Only diff between this & classic H3 is the indication of the roll at the start of the unit's turn.
  • Negative: Minor - 50% reduced movement hexes of 50% reduced atk spd for the turn. Same indication/roll as Positive outcomes. Major - Fully lost turn like H3C.

Luck

  • Like Morale, same +-3 scale as H3C except for GM Luck/Intimidation giving +-4, but positive & negative outcomes follow the same 5-outcome system as the RPG. Also, Master Luck/Intimidation effects cancel out, but GM effects override Master.
  • The Luck Factor that determines what outcome occurs is based on the attacking unit's luck minus the defending unit's luck. Possible ranges are from -8 to +8: 0 means every hit is an Average Hit. +1 opens up possibility of Critical. +3 opens up possibility of Slayer. +5 guarantees Critical, +7 guarantees Slayer, +8 increases Slayer mod by 100%. The reverse is symmetrically true.

Ranged Units

  • Ranged attacks should suffer a distance penalty (e.g. -25% dmg for firing >10 hexes away), and additionally shooting over castle walls should downgrade a hit one category (e.g. an Average Hit should become a Weak Hit) unless skills prevent it. (TBS) Ranged attacks should suffer a distance penalty & also have a max distance travelled before completely petering out & the missile disappearing, even spells. I think -50% here for long range is ok since there aren't really castle walls to fire over (RPG).
