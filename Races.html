Sentient Races & Their Playability

The division between sentience & non-sentience, as well as the distinction of who can be played a TBS "hero", RPG character, etc., vs. who is an NPC to be ordered around, is a choice laden with bias. Make all decisions, including language, consciously & critically.

Classes/Jobs

Five categories: All-Might, Might-focused, Balanced, Magic-focused, All-Magic. First is chosen specialization: depends on region, skills, stats. Beyond that are honorific titles akin to 2nd promotions in MM7C. Unlike MM7C, skill masteries should only be gated behind achieving 1st specialization, 2nd should be specialty focus, stat growth & honor. In RPG, chars pick 1 of 5 basic paths to start in, then progress to a specialty by skill choice & accomplishing a quest. Choosing 1 path locks out all others for actual promotion, but quest xp can be gained & "honorary" titles as well like MMC. In TBS, all heroes start at first specialties & can progress to 2nd via experience.

TBS mechanic thoughts: Rather than having an infinite stream of heroes available to recruit, towns/factions should be able to promote a set # of available lieutenants-in-waiting (or whatever the lore justification is) per week to heroes that can move around the map & lead an army. These values (town limit/faction limit) should be adjustable on a per-map/per-campaign basis & set to 1 per week for both at default. Another mechanic should be for a map/campaign to have a preloaded set of heroes per faction ready to deploy but after those are exhausted, fall back on the limit. Think of the H3C campaigns where all the tavern heroes are lvl 3, 5, 7, etc. to start. Additionally, towns should only be able to train heroes that are affiliated with their town by default, or, more rarely & lore-dependent, train allied heroes, e.g. the H3C RoE map where you have Castle, Rampart & Tower allied against Dungeon, the former should be able to train any of the 3 at any castle.
