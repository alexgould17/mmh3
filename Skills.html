Primary Stats

Thresholds

Soft floor is 0, soft ceiling is 500. Anything above that is padding against temp reduction. General Thresholds for learning/upgrading skills: Basic - 20 stat / Advanced - 30 stat & 4 lvl / Expert - 50 stat & 7 lvl / Master - 150 (main) or 100 (aux) stat & 10 lvl / Grandmaster - 250 (main) or 150 (aux) stat & 15 lvl.

  • Might - Physical strength. Affects physical atk dmg, what weapons melee can be equipped &
  • Endurance - Physical stamina. Affects hit points, what armor can be equipped &
  • Speed - Quickness in performing any tasks. Affects party movement speed & all recovery times.
  • Accuracy - Precision in performing any tasks. Affects physical hit chance, what bow can be equipped.
  • Intellect - Competency in thinking. Affects spell points & elemental magical prowess.
  • Personality - Personal charm. Affects self magical prowess & enemy dispensation.
  • Luck - Affects everything but especially Critical Hits

Secondary Skills

  • In general, any secondary skill can be learned by any class & is only restricted by stats, with a few exceptions (e.g. Necromancers can't learn Life Magic)
  • Rather than basing derived stats (like hp & sp) and their growth on chosen class, they should be managed almost exclusively thru primary stats, with those growing accordingly. A notable exception would be spell effects, e.g. temporary hit rate reduction due to being cursed.
  • Races should have small but noticeable effects on primary stats at character creation but should taper off in favor of chosen path-based growth.

Turn-Based Game Mechanics

Primary Skills

  • Attack (largely works the same as H3C)
  • Defense (largely works the same as H3C)
  • Spell Power (largely works the same as H3C)
  • Knowledge (somewhat different)

Secondary Skills

  • Will now scale as Basic - Advanced - Expert - Master - Grandmaster to be cohesive with RPG
  • Basic must be learned at a castle in the appropriate building, at an adventure map scholar (think H3C Witch Hut) or can be spec'd into on-the-fly if hero's home castle/specialty allows it (there will be some lore about the hero travelling with scholars to fulfill this).
  • Battle skills get learned at Blacksmith, Magic skills get learned at Mage Guilds & Other get learned at Marketplace.
  • No more RNG-based secondary skills. Experience, level & primary skills (Atk/Def for Battle, Pwr/Knw for Magic) will determine how many secondary skills & to what proficiency a hero can learn.
  • Generally, Grandmastery of skills should not be able to be learned from the castle, but from an outside scholar. Each castle should have 1-2 available, thematically appropriate, once a Metropolis is built.
  • Level 1 Heroes should start with 3 skills thematically appropriate to their type, e.g. Death Knights should have Death Magic along with 2 battle skills.

Battle

  • Archery: Basic - +10% dmg to all ranged atks; Advanced - +20% dmg to all ranged atks; Expert - +30% dmg to all ranged atks, eliminate distance penalty; Master - +50% dmg to all ranged atks, also eliminate castle wall penalty; Grandmaster - +50% dmg to all ranged atks, guaranteed Critical Hit, ranged units can shoot from point-blank range
  • Armorer: Basic - 5% all incoming dmg reduction; Advanced - 10% all incoming dmg reduction; Expert - 15% all incoming dmg reduction; Master - 20% all incoming dmg reduction. Last level 1-4 creature on a stack will survive a lethal hit 1 time per round; Grandmaster - 30% all incoming damage reduction. Last creature of any level will survive a lethal hit once per round. Example: gnolls have 5hp per unit. if they take a 13 damage hit, normally 2 will die. Master/GM Armorer will allow only 1 to die from that 1 hit (unless it's the last of a stack & enough damage was done to kill a theoretical extra in the stack, so e.g. a stack of only 2 gnolls won't survive 15 or more damage)
  • Artillery: Basic - Gives control of the ballista & defense towers to the hero; Advanced - Ballista shoots twice & ballista/towers have a 50% chance of a Critical Hit + a 12.5% chance of a Slayer Strike; Expert - Ballista & towers guaranteed Critical; Master - Keep tower shoots twice, 25% chance of Slayer Strike; Grandmaster - All towers shoot twice, guaranteed Slayer Strike
  • Ballistics: Basic - Allows hero to aim catapult at specific section of wall, 1dmg gt'd; Advanced - Allows hero to aim at drawbridge, 2dmg 50%; Expert - Allows hero to aim at outer towers, 2dmg gt'd; Master - 2 shots, 3dmg 50%; Grandmaster - Allows hero to aim at Keep, 3dmg gt'd.
  • Demoralization: Basic - -1 enemy morale; Advanced - -2 enemy morale; Expert - -3 enemy morale; Master - enemy morale has -1 ceiling (does not override troop abilities), +1 bonus to self troop morale; Grandmaster - enemy morale has -2 ceiling (DOES override troop abilities), unlocks +-4 morale ceiling for self & enemy respectively.
  • First Aid: Basic - Heals up to 50hp on a single stack every turn, Hero has control of tent; Advanced - Fully heals a stack every turn; Expert - Fully heals up to 2 stacks every turn; Master - Fully heals up to 3 stacks every turn; Grandmaster - Fully heals all stacks every turn. Additionally, provides a weak resurrection to a single stack after healing.
  • Intimidation: Basic - -1 enemy luck; Advanced - -2 enemy luck; Expert - -3 enemy luck; Master - enemy luck has -1 ceiling (does not override troop abilities), +1 bonus to self troop luck; Grandmaster - enemy luck has -2 ceiling (DOES override troop abilities), unlocks +-4 luck ceiling for self & enemy respectively.
  • Leadership: Basic - +1 self morale; Advanced - +2 self morale; Expert - +3 self morale; Master - self morale has +1 floor, -1 bonus to enemy troop morale, all troops of same alignment can be used with same-castle bonus, eliminates penalty for up to 2 alignments & 3 castle types; Grandmaster - self morale has +2 floor, -2 enemy morale unlocks +-4 morale ceiling for self & enemy, -2 enemy morale, completely eliminates alignment/castle type penalties.
  • Luck: Basic - +1 self luck; +2 self luck; Expert - +3 self luck; Master - self luck has +1 floor, -1 bonus to enemy troop luck, bonus to Critical modifier; Grandmaster - self luck has +2 floor, bonus to Critical & Slayer Strike modifiers unlocks +-4 luck ceiling for self & enemy respectively .
  • Offense: Basic - +10% melee dmg; Advanced - +20% melee dmg; Expert - +30% melee dmg; Master - +40% melee dmg, Average Hit floor; Grandmaster - +50% melee dmg, Critical Hit floor.
  • Tactics: Basic - Rearrange troops in 3-hex zone pre-combat; Advanced - 5-hex zone; Expert - 7-hex zone; Master - 9-hex zone. Guarantees 3-hex zone no matter the tactics of the opponent; Grandmaster - 11-hex zone. 5-hex guarantee. May cast 1 spell or fire 1 round of siege/artillery at any time prior to combat. When 2 masters or above of Tactics face off, each troop will be able to be moved exactly 1 time, alternating offense-defense-defense-offense. Troop order will be chosen by heroes, with GM being able to elect to use spell/artillery in place of a troop choice at any time.

Magic

  • Air Magic - varies per spell
  • Earth Magic - varies per spell
  • Fire Magic - varies per spell
  • Water Magic - varies per spell
  • Body Magic (new to TBS) - varies per spell
  • Mind Magic (new to TBS) - varies per spell
  • Spirit Magic (new to TBS) - varies per spell
  • Life Magic (new to TBS, formerly "Light Magic" in RPG) - varies per spell
  • Death Magic (new to TBS, absorbs "Necromancy", formerly "Dark Magic" in RPG - varies per spell
  • Eagle Eye: Basic - 100% chance to learn any Basic spell cast, 50% chance to learn any Advanced; Advanced - 100% chance to learn any Advanced spell cast, 50% Expert; Expert - 100% chance to learn any Expert spell cast, 25% Master; Master - 100% chance to learn any Master spell cast, 10% chance to counter any spell (cannot learn a countered spell); Grandmaster - 25% chance to counter any spell cast. For all levels: cannot learn a spell from a magic discipline not already learned.
  • Intelligence: Basic - 50% bonus to spell points; Advanced - 100% bonus to spell points; Expert - 200% bonus to spell points; Master - 300% bonus to spell points, 25% reduced casting cost for all spells; Grandmaster - 400% bonus to spell points, 50% reduced spell cost, can cast limited spells an additional time per turn.
  • Mysticism: Basic - +50% sp regen/day; Advanced - +100% sp regen/day; Expert - +150% sp regen/day; Master - +250% sp regen/day. Immune to mana drain/absorption/dampening; Grandmaster - +400% sp regen/day. Regenerate the cost of the first cast spell after every battle. When garrisoned, regenerate spell points instantly after every siege in a town with a mage guild.
  • Resistance: Basic - +10% to all magic resistances for all troops; Advanced - +20% all resistances; Expert - +30% all resistances; Master - +50% all resistances. 10% chance for immunity to an enemy spell (rolled per stack individually at cast time); Grandmaster - +75% all resistances. 20% chance for magic mirror reflection of an enemy spell (rolled same as Master resist).
  • Scholar: Basic - Teach any Basic or Advanced spells to another hero; Advanced - Teach Expert Spells; Expert - Teach Master spells; Master - Reduce knowledge requirement by 2 for taught spells, can teach basic spells to a hero that doesn't know that magic discipline; Grandmaster - Eliminate knowledge requirement for taught spells. Can teach up to advanced spells to a hero that does not know a magic discipline.
  • Sorcery: Basic - +10% dmg to direct damage spells; Advanced - +20% DD dmg; Expert - +30% DD dmg; Master - +50% DD dmg. -10% DD dmg taken by own troops; Grandmaster - +100% DD dmg. 25% DD dmg reduction to own troops.
  • Wisdom (absorbed into the knowledge stat)

Other

  • Diplomacy: Basic - Weaker monster groups more likely to join, 20% reduced surrender cost, -2 level to Library of Enlightenment; Advanced - Weaker monster groups far more likely to join, -30% surrender cost, -4 Library level Expert - Even-strength monsters more likely to join, Weaker gt'd join unless maplocked out -40% surrender cost, -6 Library level; Master - Even-strength monsters far more likely to join, Weaker gt'd flee (overrides no-flee map settings) Stronger monsters will consider joining you. -60% surrender cost; Grandmaster - Stronger monsters will strongly consider joining you. Even-strength gt'd join or flee (flee overrides no-flee map settings). -90% surrender cost.
  • Estates: Basic - +125 gold/day; Advanced - +250 gold/day; Expert - +500 gold/day; Master - +1000 gold/day; Grandmaster - +200o gold/day & +1 of the castle's 7th lvl creature resource/day (e.g. Castle is +1 gem/day). Based on the hero who has the skill's faction.
  • Learning: Basic - 10% exp bonus; Advanced - 20% exp bonus; Expert - 30% exp bonus; Master - 50% exp bonus; Grandmaster - 100% exp bonus.
  • Logistics: Basic - +10% movement over land; Advanced - +20% overland movement; Expert - +30% overland movement; Master - +40% overland movement, +2 bonus to slowest creature penalty; Grandmaster - +50% overland movement bonus, slowest creature penalty eliminated, 20% bonus from movement point increasers (e.g. stables)
  • Navigation: Basic - +50% movement by boat; Advanced - +100% boat movement; Expert - +150% boat movement; Master - +200% boat movement. Troops unaffected by whirlpools; Grandmaster - +300% boat movement. Movement points transfer between land & boat.
  • Pathfinding: Basic - 25% terrain penalty reduction; Advanced - 50% reduction; Expert - 75% reduction; Master - terrain penalty eliminated. 10% bonus on neutral terrain; Grandmaster - 25% neutral terrain bonus. Additional +25% bonus on native terrain when all troops have same native terrain. Bonuses stack linearly with Logistics.
  • Scouting: Basic - +1 hex sight radius; Advanced - +2 hex; Expert - +3 hex; Master - +4 hex. Can see exact troop numbers in current range; Grandmaster - +6 hex radius. Once a week, can backtrack hero after movement points exhausted to day's starting position.
