Chances of inflicting a status are rolled on a per-event basis unless a more serious status than can be inflicted already exists on the receiving party. Rolling a status of the same level that's already been inflicted refreshes the duration. % chances are meant to be cumulative out of a total of 100%. So e.g. if creature A has a 20/30/50% chance of inflicting Poison1/2/3 for 3 turns on an attack, then it is guaranteed to inflict a poison status (20+30+50==100%). If Creature B already has Poison2 with 1 turn left, then there's a 50% chance nothing will happen (rolling Poison1 will neither override nor refresh the duration of Poison2), 30% chance of Creature B having its Poison2 status extended to last 3 more turns (Poison2 roll refreshes duration), and a 20% chance Creature B will be upgraded to Poison3 for 3 turns (TBS example).
